Project Overview

Nord Metallics is advancing a planned Direct Reduced Iron development in Louisiana designed to contribute to the evolving metallics requirements of the North American steel sector.

The project is intended to serve a market increasingly focused on cleaner metallics inputs, stronger supply reliability and long-term industrial resilience. Its strategic location offers meaningful advantages in logistics, industrial infrastructure and market access.

Planned Development

The Louisiana project is being developed as a large-scale low-carbon metallics platform with a phased growth vision. It is designed to support the future of Electric Arc Furnace steelmaking by providing a reliable source of high-quality metallics input aligned with modern steel industry requirements.

Why Louisiana

Strategic Gulf Access

The region benefits from major maritime and inland logistics connectivity.

Industrial Infrastructure

Louisiana provides access to a well-established industrial base, port facilities and related infrastructure.

Utilities and Energy Access

The broader industrial corridor offers significant relevance for power, gas and utility support.

Market Connectivity

Its location supports access to steelmaking demand centers and regional industrial supply chains.

Why This Project Matters

The Louisiana project is more than a single industrial asset. It represents part of a broader response to the steel industry’s need for cleaner and more reliable metallics.

As EAF steelmaking continues to grow, the demand for quality metallics inputs is becoming increasingly important. Nord Metallics believes strategically positioned DRI infrastructure can play a central role in this transition.
